The back muscles, also known as the posterior musculature, are a group of muscles located in the back of the torso. They are responsible for movement and stability of the spine, as well as providing support for the upper body. Some of the key muscles in the back include:

Latissimus Dorsi: This muscle is located in the middle and lower back. It originates from the spinous processes of the lower thoracic and lumbar vertebrae, and the posterior iliac crest and sacrum and inserts on the humerus. It is responsible for extension, adduction, and medial rotation of the shoulder.

Trapezius: This muscle is a large, triangular muscle that extends from the base of the skull to the middle of the back. It is responsible for movement of the shoulder blades and for stabilizing the shoulder girdle.

Rhomboids: These muscles are located between the shoulder blades and are responsible for retracting and stabilizing the shoulder blades.

Erector spinae: This is a group of muscles that run along the spine and are responsible for maintaining the upright posture and spinal stability.
