The muscles of the shoulder are responsible for a wide range of movements, including lifting, pushing, pulling, and rotating the arm. The main muscles that make up the shoulder include:

The deltoids: This muscle group is located on the upper part of the shoulder and is responsible for lifting the arm up and out to the side. It's composed of three parts: the anterior (front), medial (middle) and posterior (rear) deltoid.

The rotator cuff muscles: This group of muscles is made up of four muscles: the supraspinatus, infraspinatus, teres minor, and subscapularis. These muscles work together to stabilize the shoulder joint and allow for smooth movement of the arm.

The pectoralis major and minor: These muscles are located in the chest and help to lift and rotate the arm.

The latissimus dorsi: This muscle is located in the back and is responsible for pulling the arm down and back.

The trapezius: This muscle is located in the upper back and is responsible for lifting the shoulder blades and rotating the shoulder.

The biceps and triceps: These muscles are located in the upper arm, the biceps are responsible for flexing the elbow and the triceps are responsible for extending the elbow.

All of these muscles work together to allow for a wide range of movements in the shoulder, and to provide support and stability to the shoulder joint. Strong shoulders can help to improve posture and can also play a role in preventing injuries.

It's also important to note that the shoulder is a complex joint, and any injury or pain in the shoulder should be evaluated by a medical professional. In order to prevent injury, it's important to maintain balance in the shoulder muscles by working on both the pushing and pulling exercises.
