The chest muscles, also known as the pectoral muscles, are located in the front of the torso and are responsible for movement in the shoulders and arms. The two main muscles in the chest are the pectoralis major and the pectoralis minor.

The pectoralis major is the larger of the two chest muscles and is responsible for movements such as pushing and pulling. It originates on the sternum and clavicle and inserts on the humerus. The pectoralis minor is a smaller muscle located underneath the pectoralis major. It originates on the third, fourth, and fifth ribs and inserts on the coracoid process of the scapula.
